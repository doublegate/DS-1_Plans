## 11.1 Premise

If forced to design the weapon with as little new physics as possible, the canonical ten-item handwavium ledger can be reduced to two. Everything else remains large-but-finite extrapolation of existing physics.

## 11.2 Minimum-handwave baseline

1. **Reactor.** A ~10¹⁶ kg asteroid-scale inventory of stable condensed matter — magnetically-stabilized neutron-star chunk or Alcubierre-cavity "mass tank" — annihilated at c² specific energy against a matter substrate over 24 hours. Sustained thermal/particle output ~3 × 10²⁷ W.

2. **Conversion.** Aneutronic-style direct conversion of charged annihilation products to a coherent relativistic particle jet. The beam is the heat sink.

3. **Beam.** Not a photon laser. Relativistic (γ ~ 2–5) hadronic jet focused by magnetic quadrupole/solenoid active optics; the 10-km "dish" is a phased magnetic aperture, not a crystal lens.

4. **Firing cycle.** 24-hour recharge matches ~10²⁷ W sustained → 10³² J buffer fill time, if buffer is c²-class.

5. **Recoil.** Station mass boosted to ≥10²⁵ kg by dense reactor core; 750 m/s recoil per shot invisible on orbital scales.

## 11.3 Residual handwavium

This reduces the ledger to:

- **(a)** A substrate with ≥ c² effective storage density, stable under magnetic confinement
- **(b)** A 10-km magnetic/plasma active optic

Everything else is extrapolation of existing physics by 10–15 orders of magnitude. The eight other entries in the handwavium ledger (`10-handwavium-ledger.md`) are not strictly needed for the reactor-weapon pair — they resurface in the other subsystems (hyperdrive, tractor, gravity plates) but the core killing capability of the station can be assembled on just two concessions.

## 11.4 Design implication

For a hard-SF variant of this spec, the reference architecture can be re-rooted on this two-concession baseline and every other subsystem either:
- Recovered on real physics (ECLSS, structural, C3, ion propulsion, Whipple shielding, MARAUDER/HPM weapons), or
- Struck from the design (artificial gravity → replace with rotating habitat bands; hyperdrive → orbital operations only; tractor → electrostatic/ablative grapple at reduced capability).

This is the intellectually honest floor of "how much new physics do you need to build a Death Star?" The answer is: two specific extrapolations, both already hinted at in active 21st-century research (condensed-matter energy density; plasma-mirror active optics).
